Athletes

Meet the world's
top surfers

John John Florence

Considered by many to be the best surfer in the world, John John Florence seemed destined for this sport. Born and raised in Hawaii, he grew up in a house facing Banzai Pipeline, a world-renowned break famous for its large waves and perfect barrels. His mother is also a surfer, so John John first got on a board at just six months old and was already surfing on his own by age five. He has won two world titles in the World Surf League (WSL) and remains one of the most inspiring surfers for the next generation.

Carissa Moore

Four-time (and current) world champion, Carissa Moore has reached the podium in 9 of the last 10 years. But her achievement goes beyond that. Carissa Moore has had to compete in an exceptionally challenging era, facing off against top Australians Tyler Wright and Stephanie Gilmore. In fact, Moore, Wright, and Gilmore have been sharing the world titles since 2007! In addition to being one of the best surfers globally, Moore is also among the most influential, having been named “Adventurer of the Yearˮ by National Geographic, “Woman of the Yearˮ by Glamour, and repeatedly recognized as “Top Female Surferˮ in Surfer's poll.

Gabriel Medina

Gabriel Medina is considered by many to be the most popular surfer on the Championship Tour. Hailing from Brazil, he is one of the driving forces behind the “Brazilian Stormˮ that has shaken up the international surf scene, making Brazil the only country able to challenge the stronghold traditionally held by Australia and the United States. In his home country, he is already a living legend of the sport, especially after 2018, when he won his second world title in the WSL and his first Pipe Masters.

Caroline Marks

Caroline Marks is the leader of the new generation of 21st-century surfers. She made her debut on the Championship Tour in 2018, at only 15 years old, she holds the record as the youngest surfer to qualify. The following year (2019), she won two CT events and was named “Rookie of the Yearˮ. 2023 has been her most accomplished season to date, in which she earned her world title on her own merits. Her life story is beautifully portrayed in the documentary “That's Carolineˮ.
